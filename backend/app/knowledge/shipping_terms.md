# Freight & Shipping Terms Guide

## Container Types

### FCL — Full Container Load
- You rent the entire container
- Best for: Large shipments (15+ CBM)
- Container sizes: 20GP, 40GP, 40HC, 45HC
- Cost: Fixed price per container regardless of fill
- Transit: Faster, fewer handlings

### LCL — Less than Container Load
- You share container with other shippers
- Best for: Small shipments (less than 15 CBM)
- Cost: Charged per CBM or per ton
- Transit: Slightly slower (consolidation/deconsolidation at port)

### Container Size Guide
- **20GP (General Purpose)**: 33 CBM, max 28 tons — good for heavy cargo
- **40GP**: 67 CBM, max 28 tons — most common
- **40HC (High Cube)**: 76 CBM, max 28 tons — tall cargo
- **45HC**: 86 CBM — maximum volume

## Air Freight
- Fastest but most expensive
- Charged by volumetric weight or actual weight (whichever higher)
- Volumetric weight = (L x W x H in cm) / 5000
- Best for: Urgent, high-value, perishable cargo

## Cargo Types

### Breakbulk
- Non-containerized cargo
- Heavy machinery, steel pipes, oversized items
- Loaded directly onto vessel

### RoRo (Roll-on Roll-off)
- Wheeled cargo (vehicles, machinery)
- Driven onto and off the vessel

### Reefer
- Refrigerated containers
- Perishables, pharmaceuticals, chemicals
- Temperature controlled: -30°C to +30°C

### Hazardous Cargo (HAZMAT)
- Dangerous goods following IMDG code
- Special documentation required
- Extra costs apply

## Key Shipping Documents

### Bill of Lading (B/L)
- Most important shipping document
- Contract between shipper and carrier
- Proof of ownership of goods
- Types: Master B/L, House B/L, Original B/L, Seaway Bill

### Commercial Invoice
- Seller issues to buyer
- Shows price, quantity, description
- Required for customs clearance

### Packing List
- Details of what's in each package
- Weight, dimensions, contents

### Certificate of Origin
- Proves where goods were manufactured
- Required for customs duty calculation
- Saudi Arabia often requires Arab League certification

### Letter of Credit (L/C)
- Bank payment guarantee
- Common in international trade
- Protects both buyer and seller

## Freight Rates & Charges

### Ocean Freight Components
- **Base Rate (BAS)**: Basic ocean freight
- **BAF**: Bunker Adjustment Factor (fuel surcharge)
- **CAF**: Currency Adjustment Factor
- **PSS**: Peak Season Surcharge (Jul-Oct)
- **GRI**: General Rate Increase

### Port Charges
- **THC**: Terminal Handling Charge — charged at both origin and destination ports
- **Origin Charges**: Documentation, seal, fumigation
- **Destination Charges**: D/O fee, customs examination

## Transit Times (Approximate)
- China → Saudi Arabia (Jeddah): 20-25 days
- China → UAE (Dubai): 18-22 days
- India → Saudi Arabia: 8-12 days
- Europe → Saudi Arabia: 20-30 days
- USA → Saudi Arabia: 25-35 days
