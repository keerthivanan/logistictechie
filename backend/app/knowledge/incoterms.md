# Incoterms 2020 — Complete Guide

## What are Incoterms?
Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) are standardized trade terms published by the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC). They define who is responsible for shipping costs, insurance, and risk between buyer and seller.

## The 11 Incoterms 2020

### EXW — Ex Works
- Seller makes goods available at their premises
- Buyer bears ALL costs and risks from seller's door
- Best for: Experienced importers who control logistics
- Example: Buyer picks up from factory in China, handles everything

### FCA — Free Carrier
- Seller delivers to named carrier at seller's premises or another named place
- Risk transfers when carrier takes possession
- Best for: Container shipments, any transport mode
- Example: Seller delivers to port/airport, buyer pays freight

### CPT — Carriage Paid To
- Seller pays freight to named destination
- Risk transfers when goods given to first carrier
- Best for: Any transport mode

### CIP — Carriage and Insurance Paid To
- Like CPT but seller must provide insurance
- Minimum insurance: Institute Cargo Clauses (A)
- Best for: High-value goods

### DAP — Delivered At Place
- Seller delivers to named place at destination
- Buyer responsible for import duties and customs
- Best for: Buyers who prefer goods at door

### DPU — Delivered at Place Unloaded
- Seller delivers AND unloads at named destination
- Seller bears all risks until unloaded
- Best for: When unloading is critical

### DDP — Delivered Duty Paid
- Maximum obligation for seller
- Seller handles everything including import duties
- Best for: Buyers with no import experience

### FAS — Free Alongside Ship
- Seller delivers goods alongside vessel at named port
- Sea and inland waterway only
- Best for: Bulk cargo, heavy machinery

### FOB — Free On Board (Most Common in Middle East/Asia trade)
- Seller loads goods onto vessel at named port
- Risk transfers when goods cross ship's rail
- Best for: FCL ocean freight, bulk cargo
- Very common in Saudi Arabia, UAE imports from China

### CFR — Cost and Freight
- Seller pays cost and freight to destination port
- Risk transfers when goods loaded on vessel
- Buyer arranges insurance
- Best for: Ocean freight

### CIF — Cost, Insurance and Freight (Very common)
- Seller pays cost, freight AND insurance to destination port
- Most common for bulk cargo and commodities
- Very common for Middle East imports
- Example: CIF Jeddah, CIF Dubai

## Which Incoterm to Choose?
- **Importing to Saudi Arabia from China**: FOB or CIF most common
- **Air freight**: CPT or CIP recommended
- **First-time importer**: DDP (seller handles everything)
- **Experienced importer**: FOB or EXW (more control, lower cost)
- **High value goods**: CIP (insurance included)
