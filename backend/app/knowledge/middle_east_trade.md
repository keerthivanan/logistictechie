# Middle East Trade & Customs Guide

## Saudi Arabia (KSA) Import Rules

### Key Facts
- Currency: Saudi Riyal (SAR)
- Main ports: Jeddah Islamic Port, King Abdulaziz Port (Dammam), Jubail Commercial Port
- Main airports: Riyadh (RUH), Jeddah (JED), Dammam (DMM)
- Customs authority: ZATCA (Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority)

### Customs Duty Rates
- Most goods: 5% customs duty
- Food products: 0-5%
- Vehicles: 5%
- Tobacco: 100%
- Alcohol: Prohibited
- GCC countries: 0% (Gulf Cooperation Council free trade)

### Required Documents for KSA Import
1. Commercial Invoice (original + 3 copies)
2. Packing List
3. Bill of Lading or Airway Bill
4. Certificate of Origin (Arab League certified if from Arab countries)
5. Insurance Certificate
6. SASO Certificate (for regulated products)
7. Halal Certificate (for food products)

### Prohibited Items in Saudi Arabia
- Alcohol and narcotics
- Pork products
- Items contrary to Islamic faith
- Weapons without permit
- Certain chemicals

### VAT
- 15% VAT on all imports
- VAT registration required for businesses

## UAE Import Rules

### Key Facts
- Currency: UAE Dirham (AED)
- Main ports: Jebel Ali (world's 9th largest), Port Rashid, Khalifa Port
- Free Zones: JAFZA, DAFZA, DMCC — 0% customs duty
- Customs authority: Federal Customs Authority

### Customs Duty
- Standard rate: 5%
- Free zone imports: 0%
- Tobacco: 100%
- Alcohol: 50% (permitted in licensed areas)
- GCC: 0%

### Free Zones Advantage
- 100% foreign ownership
- 0% import/export duties
- No personal income tax
- Popular: JAFZA (Jebel Ali), Dubai Airport Free Zone

## Tips for Shippers
1. Book 2-3 weeks in advance for FCL
2. LCL shipments need 5-7 days extra for consolidation
3. Ramadan period: expect delays in Middle East customs
4. National Day holidays: Saudi (Sep 23), UAE (Dec 2-3)
5. Always get Certificate of Origin — required at Saudi customs
6. Use FOB or CIF terms — most common in Middle East trade
